# Community code of conduct

## Our standard

This project welcomes respectful, technically grounded participation. In all
project spaces, contributors are expected to:

- discuss ideas and behavior without attacking people;
- give useful context, reproduction steps, and evidence;
- respect differing experience levels, identities, backgrounds, and viewpoints;
- accept correction, acknowledge mistakes, and keep review focused on the work;
- protect other people's credentials, personal data, prompts, and media;
- avoid harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized attention, spam, and
  deliberate disruption.

## Scope

This standard applies to repository Issues, pull requests, reviews, and other
spaces where someone is representing this project. It applies equally to
maintainers, contributors, and users.

## Reporting and enforcement

For conduct in GitHub-hosted project spaces, use the repository's Issues only
when a calm, minimally identifying public report is safe. Use GitHub's platform
reporting tools for abusive content or accounts. Do not place private messages,
contact details, or sensitive personal information in a public Issue.

Maintainers may edit or remove harmful content and may warn, temporarily limit,
or permanently exclude participants whose behavior makes the project unsafe or
unworkable. Decisions should be proportionate, based on observable behavior,
and avoid unnecessary public disclosure. Community moderation is best effort;
no response deadline is promised.

Good-faith disagreement about code, architecture, model provenance, or risk is
not itself misconduct. Retaliation against someone who raises a security,
privacy, accessibility, or conduct concern is unacceptable.
